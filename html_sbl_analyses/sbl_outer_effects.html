Language-Specific Outer Effect Curves

This page visualizes the Outer Effect Curves for each language. For a fixed total dependent count $n \in \{2, 3, 4\}$, we plot the average constituent size of the dependent at visual distance $k \in \{1..n\}$ from the verb.

  • Right Side ($R_n^k$): Visual distance $k$ increases from left to right (from $k=1$ closest to the verb, to $k=4$ furthest).
  • Left Side ($L_n^k$): Visual distance $k$ increases towards the left, meaning that $k=1$ (closest to the verb) is on the right, and $k=4$ (furthest from the verb) is on the left. The Left graph's x-axis is inverted to match this physical layout.
  • Shared Scale: To allow direct visual comparison of the Left and Right effect strengths, both plots for the same language share identical X and Y axis limits.
